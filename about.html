About me

I built my first website in the late 90's armed with a copy of AOLPress and a burning desire to be part of the internet craze that was taking off. It was awful - think embedded Java Applets and auto-playing midi files. Since then I've managed to keep up the enthusiasm for development and turn a hobby into a 7 year career as a front and back-end developer.

I've coded sites in Coldfusion, PHP and ASP.net as well as built and configured ones powered by CMS software like Drupal and WordPress. As well as a developer I have acted as technical lead on a number of projects as well as a project manager, often in roles that involve explaining complex concepts to people who are non-technical.

At the end of 2013 I took the opportunity to take a year out from my career and go travelling. I'm currently in Hong Kong.

Currently reading

I'm currently reading JavaScript: The Good parts by Douglas Crockford.

My set up

I'm currently on the road and so my development set up consists of windows laptop running Sublime Text 2 with Mixture.io for SASS compilation and previewing and Fireworks for image editing.

My philosophy to front-end development

I've spent most of my career supporting legacy browsers, a fact that has left a lasting mark on how I approach site builds. As a result I'm a big fan of progressive enchancement and making sure that content can be delivered to the widest audience as possible.
